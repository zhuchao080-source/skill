## Core Rule

IP style controls mood, palette, scene, prop language, copy tone, and layout rhythm. It must not override product structure, confirmed SKU color, official artwork placement, package shape, or claim-source rules.

The IPs covered by this skill are assumed to be authorized product projects. Codex may use the named IP, its visual world, character mood, official-style title hierarchy, and supplied or visible IP artwork as part of the design direction. Still preserve exact supplied artwork, logo position, product print area, and package faces. Do not invent legal small print, authorization codes, barcodes, manufacturer text, unprovided specifications, or unsupported bundle contents.

Choose the final style by matching three things together: the IP, the product's own category tone, and the target audience. A good direction should explain why the scene, palette, prop level, and text layout fit all three. Do not apply a generic cute style to every IP product, and do not make the product look like a toy-only display when the buyer also needs to understand function, portability, comfort, or performance.

## IP Source And Authorization

Classify IP input before planning:

- **Authorized Skill IP**: Sanrio, Crayon Shin-chan, PINGU, Disney, tokidoki, Brunch Brothers, Coca-Cola, or another IP the user says is authorized for this skill.
- **Confirmed IP**: user states the IP name and supplies product/package images or official artwork.
- **Visible IP**: IP graphic, logo, package style, or character artwork is visible on the provided product/package.
- **Style reference only**: user provides a mood/reference image but not official product artwork.
- **Unconfirmed IP**: user mentions a general vibe outside the skill's authorized IP scope and does not confirm licensing or provide official graphics.

Rules:

- For authorized Skill IPs, the design may use the IP name, character world, palette, packaging rhythm, and title hierarchy. Exact official logos, character artwork, and legal marks should come from supplied product/package/artwork when accuracy matters.
- Do not generate authorization codes, copyright lines, barcodes, manufacturer text, official seals, or legal small print unless the user supplies exact source content.
- Use "正版授权" or similar authorization wording only when the user asks for it or when it is part of the supplied product/package/brief. Do not invent a license number, licensor name, or legal statement.
- Do not add extra characters, logos, slogans, bundle gifts, or co-brand claims beyond the authorized IP direction and the current product's supplied/visible scope.
- When the product already contains official IP artwork, preserve its position, scale, orientation, color, and print-area boundary.
- When only an IP mood is requested, describe the scene as "inspired by the product's cute/playful/retro/pop style" rather than copying protected character designs.
- Small PVC-style figurine props are allowed only as scene accents when they fit the target audience and do not imply an official bundle accessory.

## Target Audience Fit

Choose scene and layout from the likely buyer:

- **Young female / gift buyer**: warm bedroom, vanity desk, soft blanket, gift box, ribbon, pastel packaging, gentle light, tidy composition.
- **Girl-cute IP buyer / 少女风可爱人群**: soft pastel or clean high-key scene, cute desktop, bedroom gift setup, vanity table, collectible shelf, warm light, rounded cards, small PVC material figure accents, gentle decorative labels.
- **Students / dorm users**: study desk, backpack, stationery, small shelf, nightstand, compact storage, bright practical labels.
- **Office / commuter users**: clean desk, tote bag, phone, tablet, laptop, cable organization, restrained layout, clear benefit blocks.
- **Family / parent-child gift**: bright safe-feeling scene, clear package/list, friendly colors, simple text, no clutter.
- **Collector / IP fan**: product-first display, package and accessories, color collection, neat shelf or acrylic-display feeling, stronger brand mood but no fake official text.
- **Youth street / trend buyer**: bolder color blocks, stickers, playful badges, dynamic diagonals, higher graphic density while keeping text readable.

For Sanrio, Crayon Shin-chan, PINGU, Disney, tokidoki, and Brunch Brothers style products, the default ecommerce direction may be 少女风, 可爱, giftable, collectible, and soft lifestyle when the target buyer is young female, student, gift buyer, or IP fan. Match target audience to product price and use frequency. A small daily electronic product should still feel practical and product-clear, not only decorative.

Use this fit check before writing the direction package:

1. Name the IP mood: cute, cool-cute, comic-playful, premium gift, pop-collector, warm healing, refreshment, or another clear mood.
2. Name the product tone: daily carry, desk use, beauty care, audio listening, travel charging, compact storage, gift set, or collector display.
3. Name the buyer: young female, student, commuter, office user, gift buyer, parent-child buyer, collector, youth trend buyer, or practical daily user.
4. Choose one combined style sentence, such as `PINGU controlled clash-color cool-cute + travel/daily carry + student/gift buyer`.
5. Reject any style that satisfies only the IP but not the product use or buyer concern.

## IP Color Strategy

Use the product/IP palette as the base, then add color in a controlled way:

- Add neighboring analogous colors to scene backgrounds, cards, props, ribbons, soft shadows, and page transitions.
- Add controlled clash-color / contrast-color pairings for stronger ecommerce impact. Use them for title boards, background blocks, cards, pedestals, stickers, arrows, badges, icon dots, speech bubbles, tiny labels, or decorative highlights.
- Every generated scene should include at least two clearly different color families. Examples: blue+yellow, pink+mint, red+cyan, purple+yellow, cream+blue, orange+green, or black/white product contrast plus a warm/cool scene color. Do not build an entire screen from only one color family.
- Keep clash/contrast colors purposeful. They should guide the eye and add impact, not compete with the product or damage IP recognition.
- Do not recolor real SKU colors, product artwork, official logo areas, package faces, or accessories to fit the page palette.
- Keep scene props lower contrast than the product unless they are tiny accent objects.
- For girl-cute IP products, analogous pastels can create softness; clash colors such as pink+mint, cream+blue, or purple+yellow can add impact to title cards, props, and labels.
- For pop/trend IP products, clash colors can be stronger, but product and copy readability remain higher priority.

## IP Style Profiles

### Sanrio

Use cute, soft, girl-cute, giftable, clean styling. Prefer milk white, soft pink, red-white, baby blue, pale yellow, and gentle cream backgrounds depending on the character/SKU. Scenes can include bedroom gift setup, vanity desk, soft blanket, tidy desktop, cute stationery, ribbon, small mirror, clean package display, and small glossy PVC figure accents.

Layout should be sweet but orderly: rounded cards, soft shadows, small hearts/stars/ribbons only when they do not cover the product. Copy tone is gentle, giftable, daily-use, and easy to understand.

### Crayon Shin-chan

Use playful, energetic, cute, family-friendly, comic-life styling. Prefer warm yellow, orange, red, sky blue, green, and bold simple color blocks. Scenes can include school bag, snack table, living room floor, casual desk, playful stickers, small PVC figure accents, and motion-like graphic accents.

Layout can be more dynamic with tilted labels and comic panels, but product structure and text readability stay strict. Copy tone can be witty and lively without using official catchphrases unless supplied.

### PINGU

Use cool-cute, playful, giftable, daily-electronics styling. Prefer shallow blue, sky blue, or baby blue as the main PINGU page/frame/title base, then choose one controlled clash family from yellow, red/orange, pink, purple, cream/warm white, or black/white neutrals according to SKU color, product category, and target buyer. Keep small red/orange memory accents from PINGU artwork when useful. Do not use green, mint, teal-green, grass green, dark navy main fields, ice, snow, glaciers, igloos, or winter-only worlds unless explicitly requested.

Scenes can include desk, room, shelf, gift box, package set, tote/backpack, pouch, summer daily-use, product-on-base display, and daily-carry setups. For rendered product screens, use round pedestals, paper boards, tape corners, torn-paper edges, dotted texture, sticker outlines, cards, notebooks, clouds, stars, music notes, and soft shadows. Build the same density and buyer appeal as Sanrio or Crayon Shin-chan IP pages: product first, clear title hierarchy, visible props, foreground/midground/background depth, and no empty clean catalog pedestal.

Layout should be product-first and layered: large paper-board or notebook-card title at the top or upper-left, smaller subtitle strip, 2-4 stickers or badges, and clear foreground/midground/background props. Use product position, scale, height, overlap, high/low pedestals, and front/back staggering to create visual guidance. Do not draw obvious guide lines when the user asks for natural visual guidance.

For earphones, power banks, stands, speakers, fans, and compact accessories, use product-led 3D/cartoon-rendered scenes with confirmed SKU colors and angles. For blow dryers and beauty/use appliances, use a realistic lifestyle model hero when the first screen needs impact: model behind the product, hand pushing the product forward, product closest to camera, and structure clearly visible.

Preserve visible PINGU/Pingu text and product-surface lettering. Do not copy MarTUBE/store text, official wordmark shapes, legal text, QR codes, slogans, commercial fonts, or unsupported numeric claims.

### Disney

Use polished, magical, cute, family-friendly, premium gift styling. Palette depends on the specific product artwork; common safe directions include warm cream, soft gold, classic red/black/white accents, gentle blue, or fairytale pastel.

Scenes can include premium gift box, bedroom shelf, family gift setting, soft spotlight, clean hero composition, small PVC figure accents, and subtle sparkle-like accents. Do not invent character silhouettes, castle marks, official logos, or movie references unless supplied.

### tokidoki

Use colorful, pop, street-cute, girl-cute, collector-oriented styling. Prefer high-saturation pink, cyan, purple, yellow, mint, and sticker-like color accents. Scenes can include acrylic display, toy shelf, sticker collage, vivid desktop, pouch, collectible packaging, small PVC figure accents, and energetic card modules.

Layout may use playful density, sticker labels, and layered color blocks, but keep product and copy from becoming buried. Use clean generic fonts with colorful containers rather than named commercial display fonts.

### Brunch Brothers

Use warm, playful, cute, food-inspired, friendly styling. Prefer cream, toast brown, butter yellow, soft green, orange, and clean white. Scenes can include breakfast table, cozy desk, cafe-like props, bread/pancake-shaped soft props, napkin, plate, cup, small PVC figure accents, and warm morning light.

Layout should feel relaxed, neat, and product-first. Avoid making food props larger than the product unless the product itself is a food-themed collectible.

### Coca-Cola

Use refreshing, retro-pop, red-white, bright, energetic styling. Prefer classic red, white, black, silver, and cool condensation highlights. Scenes can include clean red-white display, summer desk, cooler-inspired props, glass/reflection, ice-like transparent blocks, and high-contrast graphic cards.

Do not add the Coca-Cola logo, script lettering, bottle/can artwork, official slogans, or brand claims unless supplied. Use the color system and refreshment mood safely.

## PVC Figure Accent Rules

Small PVC material figures may be used as decorative props for Sanrio, Crayon Shin-chan, PINGU, Disney, tokidoki, Brunch Brothers, and similar IP-style product scenes when they fit the target buyer's taste.

Use them like this:

- Keep them small and secondary; the product remains the hero.
- Place them near the background edge, on a shelf, beside the packaging, or as a low-contrast desk prop.
- Give them glossy PVC material, rounded edges, soft highlight, toy-collectible scale, and clean contact shadow.
- Match their colors and mood to the product/IP direction.
- For authorized Skill IPs, small matching IP figurine accents may be used when they fit the product and target buyer, but they must remain secondary and must not imply included accessories unless confirmed.
- Use exact official/supplied character shapes when the user provides them, when they are visible on the product/package, or when the authorized IP brief explicitly calls for them. If exactness matters, reserve the figure area for official assets.
- Do not list PVC figures as included accessories unless the user confirms they are part of the package.
- Do not cover ports, buttons, screens, vents, fold lines, nozzles, product logo, package front, or text areas.
- Avoid turning the scene into a toy display where the product becomes secondary.

For prompt writing, describe them as `small glossy PVC-style decorative figurine props matching the IP mood, secondary to the product, no extra official logos or unsupplied character details`.

## Scene Generation Rules

Choose scenes from the product category and IP mood together:

- Power bank: travel bag, desk charging, bedside, emergency backup, gift display.
- Earphones: commute pouch, desk listening, gift set, hand-held case, package collection.
- Stand: desk, phone/tablet viewing, dorm, bedside, office, live-stream setup.
- Charger/cable: clean charging station, cable organization, travel pouch, desk setup.
- Speaker: desk, bedroom, picnic, shelf display, cozy listening scene.
- Fan/lamp/blow dryer: bedroom, vanity, dorm, bathroom counter, travel packing, gift scene.

Props must support the target user and IP tone. Keep props lower contrast than the product. Do not let props hide ports, buttons, screens, hinges, cables, nozzles, or package details. For girl-cute IP scenes, PVC-style figure props can be used with soft blankets, vanity objects, stationery, collectible shelves, gift boxes, and pastel desktop props.

When a product category has a practical buyer concern, include it visually even in a cute IP page. Earphones should still show wearing or case storage. Blow dryers should still show grip, nozzle, folding, or hair-care use. Power banks should still show charging, ports, travel bag, or daily carry. Stands should still show support angle, phone/tablet relation, folding, or desk use.

Choose rendering style by how much the product depends on a person in the first visual:

- **Low-model IP electronics**: earphones, power banks, stands, chargers/cables, speakers, and compact desk accessories can use a product-led 3D/cartoon-rendered scene. Use soft IP-colored backgrounds, rounded pedestals, cards, desk/shelf/gift props, pouch/backpack props, and clean product display. Let product placement create the visual path: high/low bases, front/back depth, overlap, staggered SKUs, and product scale. Do not add obvious guide lines, arrows, glowing trails, or decorative curves when the user asks for natural visual guidance through placement.
- **Person-heavy IP appliances**: blow dryers and similar beauty/use products should use a more realistic lifestyle model scene when the first screen needs impact. The model should appear as a use-proof element, not the main product. For a blow-dryer hero, place the model behind or beside the product, extend the hand toward camera, and make the dryer large, close, and dominant. The composition should feel dynamic and tense enough that the buyer notices the product first.
- **Mixed IP pages**: use realistic model scenes for hero/use proof when needed, then return to product-rendered scenes for structure, core grid, detail crop, packaging, SKU, and parameter screens.

Blow-dryer first-screen prompt requirements:

- Model出镜 in a warm lifestyle room, vanity, dorm, or bathroom-counter scene that fits the IP and buyer.
- Hand/arm reaches forward holding the dryer, creating strong foreground/background depth.
- Dryer is closest to camera and visually dominant; model face/body is secondary.
- Show barrel, air outlet/nozzle area, handle, buttons, IP artwork, fold joint, and cord/accessory state clearly.
- Use stronger perspective and visual tension, but avoid fisheye, hand deformation, blocked product structures, or toy-like redesign.

## Typography And Text Safety

The user rule "文字禁止使用商用文字" means:

- Do not name or require commercial fonts, paid fonts, or proprietary brand fonts.
- Authorized IP title/logo treatment may be planned when the IP project requires it, but final production should use supplied official assets or license-safe editable type. Do not fabricate distorted brand wordmarks or pretend AI-rendered raster text is a final official logo.
- Use license-safe generic descriptions such as `clean rounded sans-serif`, `simple bold sans-serif`, `soft handwritten-style generic lettering`, or `geometric sans-serif with rounded corners`.
- Prefer editable text layers or reserved text areas when final commercial typography must be applied later.
- Do not add official slogans, catchphrases, licensing statements, barcode text, authorization text, manufacturer text, or certification text unless supplied.
- Keep visible selling copy original, short, and product-specific.

Chinese copy should use natural ecommerce language and avoid absolute promises. For AI-generated raster text, keep text short and plan manual replacement when accuracy matters.

## Audit Checklist

Check before approval or delivery:

- Style direction explicitly matches IP, product category tone, and target audience.
- IP mood matches the product and target audience.
- Product structure remains unchanged.
- Authorized IP direction is used intentionally and matches the product/category/audience.
- Exact official artwork, logos, and title marks are preserved or reserved for supplied assets when production accuracy matters.
- No legal small print, authorization code, barcode, manufacturer text, unsupported slogan, bundle gift, or co-brand claim was invented.
- Typography direction avoids commercial font names and unreliable AI-rendered official wordmarks.
- Scene props support use case and do not cover product structure.
- Color uses the product/IP base palette, neighboring analogous colors, controlled clash/contrast colors, and at least two different color families per generated scene.
- Contrast accents do not overpower the product or reduce copy readability.
- PVC-style figure props, when used, are small secondary accents and do not imply included accessories.
- Layout density fits the IP: soft and clean for gift/cute products, higher energy for pop/trend products, restrained for premium/family products.
- Copy is original, short, readable, and does not use absolute or unsupported claims.
